Here's a timeline of LeBron James' NBA career milestones and achievements:

  • 2003-2004 - He was selected by the Cleveland Cavaliers with the first overall pick of the Draft. He finished the season averaging 20.9 points, 5.5 rebounds and 5.9 assists per game. At the conclusion of the season he received the NBA Rookie of the Year Award. The Cavs' failed to make the Playoffs with a 35-47 record.
  • 2004-2008 - After a promising first year, he achieved superstar status in the following years receiving his first all-NBA selection in his sophomore season, improving his stats year by year and leading the Cleveland Cavaliers to the 2007 NBA Finals where they faced the 2 time champions San Antonio Spurs. The Cavs' eventually got swept 4-0 by the Spurs. He also won the All Star Game MVP both in 2006 and 2008 and claimed the scoring title in 2008.
  • 2008-2010 - At the end of the 2008-2009 season, he finished second in NBA Defensive Player of the Year Award voting and made his first NBA All-Defensive Team. Behind his play Cleveland went a franchise record 66-16. He became the first Cavalier to win the MVP Award. The following year he picked up right where he left off winning his second MVP Award and leading the Cavaliers to the first seed in the 2010 NBA Playoffs. Cleveland eventually lost 4-2 in the Eastern Conference Semifinals to the Boston Celitcs.
  • 2010-2014 - After becoming a free agent in the summer of 2010 LeBron decided to join the Miami Heat to form the Big 3 together with Chris Bosh and Dwyane Wade, a decision he was highly criticized for. The first season with the Heat came to an abrupt conclusion losing the NBA Finals against the Dallas Mavericks 4-2. The next year James won his third NBA MVP Award and was finally able to lead his team to the NBA Championship for the second time in franchise history, in the Finals the Heat closed out the Oklahoma City Thunder in 5 games, LeBron averaged 28.6 points, 10.2 rebounds and 7.4 assists as he was unanimously voted the Bill Russell NBA Finals MVP. The Heat repeated as champions in the 2012-2013 season by beating the San Antonio Spurs 4-3 in the Finals, James was named the MVP of both the Finals and the Regular Season for the second straight time. In the 2013-2014 Season the Heat were able to get to the championship round for the 4th time in a row but came up short as they were defeated by the Spurs 4-1 in the NBA Finals.
  • 2014-2018 - In the summer of 2014 James decided to go back to the Cleveland Cavaliers to fulfill the promise of delivering a championship to the city of Cleveland. Thanks to the experience gained throughout his tenure as a Heat and a roster that featured two other All-Stars in Kyrie Irving and Kevin Love, he was able to lead his team to the NBA Finals in his first season back with the Cavs, although injuries deflated the Cavaliers dreams of winning a championship as they were defeated 4-2 by the Golden State Warriors. In the 2015-2016 season LeBron and the Cavs went back to the NBA Finals where they claimed the trophy with a historic comeback after trailing in the series 3 games to 1 against the Warriors, The city of Cleveland ended a 52 year championship drought and LeBron James was named MVP of the Finals for the third time in his career, the series provided a few iconic moments like "The Block" by James on Iguodala and "The Shot" by his fellow teammate Kyrie Irving to put the Cavs in front in the final minute of game 7. The 2016-2017 and 2017-2018 seasons ended with 2 other trips to the Finals where the Cavaliers lost 4-1 and 4-0 respectively against the Warriors.
  • 2018-present - After the 2018 season James joined the Los Angeles Lakers, in his first season as a Laker he suffered a groin injury which sidelined him for a good chunk of the season as the Lakers weren't able to qualify for the NBA Playoffs. In the 2019-2020 season a healthy LeBron and Anthony Davis led the Lakers to the Western Conference first seed in the Regular Season before the season was suspended because of Covid-19. The 2019-2020 season is scheduled to restart on July 30th 2020.
